Remove Spark ButtonBar Selection Toggle Behaviour

One thing that consistently annoys me is the behaviour of the Spark ButtonBar. Without any customisation, clicking one of the buttons on a Spark ButtonBar, puts said Button into it’s selected state. Conversely, the Halo ButtonBar has the opposite – and should I say more desirable – effect, of not gluing the buttons state to selected.

[SWF]/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/SparkButtonBarOldBehaviour.swf, 350, 100[/SWF]

It got me thinking as to how best to overcome this behaviour – or better still, give the user the option of having it behave in either manner.

My initial though was simply to create a new ButtonBarButtonSkin, which could ignore the ‘selectedStates’ group. But this would require a ButtonBarFirstButtonSkin, ButtonBarMiddleButtonSkin, ButtonBarLastButtonSkin and the parent ButtonBarSkin! Phew, too much work, and too obtrusive.

The second option was to simply listen to the ButtonBar’s IndexChangeEvent and set the selectedIndex back to -1 everytime a button was clicked. Given the three locations I might perform this:

Outside of the component by listening to the ButtonBar’s change event,
Extending ButtonBar and hijacking the commitSelection method to revert the selection, or
Changing the Skins state, in the skin, depending on the ‘requireSelection’ property
I chose to go with the later, as it seemed the cleanest, most unobtrusive way, requiring the least amount of work, and the most reusable.

By listening for the ‘change’ event on the hostComponent in ButtonBarSkin, we could toggle the behaviour based on the ‘requireSelection’ property. When requireSelection = true, the ButtonBar behaves with it’s usual Spark behaviour. When requireSelection = false, it would adopt the legacy Halo behaviour.
